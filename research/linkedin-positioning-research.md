# LinkedIn Positioning Research

Date: 2026-03-26

## Goal
Reposition profile/career narrative from `mobile developer / iOS developer` to a broader AI/agentic builder identity without losing the strong Apple-platform credibility.

The target outcome is:
- companies can find the profile through current market terms
- the profile accurately reflects current work
- Apple-platform expertise remains a differentiator, not the whole identity

## Core Recommendation
Do **not** lead with:
- `iOS Developer`
- `Mobile Developer`
- `Agentic Developer`

Use a primary title that is already legible in the market, then layer in the differentiators.

Best primary terms:
1. `Applied AI Engineer`
2. `AI Product Engineer`
3. `Founding AI Engineer`
4. `AI Engineer`
5. `Agent Engineer` as a secondary keyword, not the main title

## Recommended Headline Direction
Best balanced option:
- `Applied AI Engineer | Agentic Systems Builder | iOS & macOS Expert`

Alternative startup/founder version:
- `Founding AI Engineer | Agentic Systems | iOS & macOS Expert`

Alternative product-heavy version:
- `AI Product Engineer | Agentic Systems | iOS & macOS Expert`

## Why This Direction
### Why not `Agentic Developer`
- understandable, but still weak as a standard searchable title
- reads more like a personal branding phrase than an established hiring keyword

### Why `Applied AI Engineer`
- already used in the market
- broad enough to cover real system building, not just research
- compatible with agent systems, retrieval, orchestration, and product work

### Why keep `iOS & macOS Expert`
- it preserves a clear hard-skill differentiator
- it signals depth, not just hype
- it supports the positioning that this is someone who can build real products end-to-end, including polished Apple-platform experiences

## LinkedIn Strategy
### Headline
Use one strong searchable title plus differentiators.

Recommended:
- `Applied AI Engineer | Agentic Systems Builder | iOS & macOS Expert`

### About Section
The About section should say:
- builds AI/agent systems end-to-end
- combines product thinking, systems thinking, and UX
- also brings deep Apple-platform expertise
- can build full products, not just prompts or prototypes

### Experience Section
Do not frame current work primarily as mobile engineering.
Frame it around:
- agent systems
- applied AI products
- product building
- founder/product direction if relevant

### Skills / Keywords
Important keyword set to include across profile and resume:
- Applied AI
- AI Product Engineering
- AI Agents
- Agentic Systems
- LLMs
- RAG
- Prompt Engineering
- Swift
- Python
- iOS Development
- macOS Development
- Product Engineering
- System Design

## Suggested Positioning Summary
A good short framing is:

`Applied AI engineer building agentic systems and AI-native products, with deep expertise in iOS and macOS.`

That is much stronger than being positioned only as a mobile engineer, while still preserving the Apple-platform edge.

## Notes For Website Integration
This positioning should likely influence:
- homepage hero copy
- short bio
- resume summary
- LinkedIn headline
- project descriptions

The site should present the Apple-platform work as proof of engineering depth, not as the entire category.

## Reference Signals
The reasoning here was based on current market usage of titles like:
- `Applied AI Engineer`
- `AI Product Engineer`
- `Founding AI Engineer`

These appear to be stronger search terms than `agentic developer` while still fitting the actual work.
