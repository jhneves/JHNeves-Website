Salesforce Mobile

Long-horizon platform work inside a massive mobile product, spanning architecture, tooling, hybrid systems, and product-enabling infrastructure.

Salesforce - Staff Software Engineer - 2014-2022

App Store Website

Overview

Salesforce Mobile was long-horizon platform work inside a huge product that still had to keep shipping continuously. A lot of the value came from improving the foundation without forcing the rest of the organization to slow down.

The work spanned architecture, framework refactors, hybrid systems, CI/CD, automation, and developer tooling. It was the kind of engineering that makes a large app easier to change a year from now, not just easier to demo today.

What I Owned

I owned platform and framework work that sat underneath many teams: refactors, adoption work, release infrastructure, hybrid systems, and tooling that reduced friction for mobile engineers working in a large codebase.

Because the app had to keep shipping, a lot of the job was sequencing change so product teams could keep moving while the foundations were being improved underneath them.

Visible Outcomes

Several ideas that came out of this stretch later turned into patents, including work around telemetry, authentication, visual feedback, and application-state detection. Those were outputs of real platform problems, not side projects, and the patent list on the homepage comes directly out of this period.

Just as important, this was where I learned how to make platform work legible to product teams and useful at org scale, which is still a big part of how I operate.
